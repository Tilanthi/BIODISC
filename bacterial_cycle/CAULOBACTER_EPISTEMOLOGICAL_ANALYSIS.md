# Caulobacter Asymmetric Division: Epistemological Analysis and Reframing

**Date:** 2026-04-23  
**Purpose:** Address peer review concern about conflating correlation with mechanism in Caulobacter physical asymmetry claims

---

## Executive Summary

The peer review identifies a critical issue: Section 4.1's "Physical Aspects of Asymmetry" subsection discusses physical cues (PopZ curvature, DivJ/PleC localization, surface attachment) without adequately distinguishing between **correlations** and **established sensing mechanisms**. This risks overstating the evidence for physical contributions to Caulobacter asymmetry.

**Key Finding:** The hierarchical framework applies even MORE strongly to Caulobacter when we acknowledge the uncertainty about physical inputs. The molecular phosphorelay clearly creates asymmetry DE NOVO from symmetric initial conditions, demonstrating molecular override regardless of whether physical cues exist. This transforms Caulobacter from a potentially misleading example to a **model of epistemological care** that actually STRENGTHENS the hierarchical framework.

## Part 1: Evidence Categorization for Physical Aspects

### 1.1 Membrane Curvature + PopZ

**Correlations Documented:**
- PopZ forms polymeric structures at cell poles (Bowman et al., 2019)
- Poles have high membrane curvature
- PopZ localization correlates with curved regions

**Causal Evidence:**
- **WEAK/ABSENT**: No studies demonstrate that PopZ actively detects curvature
- Lasker & Gitai (2022) explicitly caution: "simple localization to curved regions does not constitute a sensing mechanism"
- Alternative explanations: PopZ may accumulate at poles due to other properties (oligomerization, interaction with polar components)

**Epistemic Status:**
- **Established**: Correlation between PopZ and curved membrane
- **Speculative**: Active curvature sensing mechanism
- **Unknown**: Whether curvature causes PopZ localization or both correlate with a third factor

**Recommendation:** Frame as "correlation" not "sensing." Distinguish between "PopZ is at curved poles" (fact) and "PopZ senses curvature" (speculative).

---

### 1.2 DivJ/PleC Localization

**Correlations Documented:**
- DivJ localizes to stalked pole (convex curvature) (Gora et al., 2023)
- PleC localizes to swarmer pole (concave curvature) (Curtis & Brun, 2022)
- Localization patterns correlate with membrane curvature type

**Causal Evidence:**
- **LIMITED**: Some studies show curvature affects localization in vitro
- **INCOMPLETE**: No conclusive demonstration that curvature sensing drives asymmetric localization in vivo
- **Alternative explanations**: Protein-protein interactions, lipid microdomain localization, existing polarity landmarks

**Epistemic Status:**
- **Established**: Asymmetric localization pattern
- **Speculative**: Curvature as primary determinant
- **Unknown**: Relative contributions of curvature vs. molecular landmarks

**Recommendation:** Present as "curvature correlates with localization" not "curvature determines localization." Acknowledge molecular landmark contributions.

---

### 1.3 Surface Attachment

**Correlations Documented:**
- Surface attachment influences initial symmetry breaking (Jenson et al., 2022)
- Stalked cells are surface-attached
- Attachment correlates with cell fate specification

**Causal Evidence:**
- **MODERATE**: Disrupting attachment affects asymmetry establishment
- **INCOMPLETE**: Mechanistic pathway from attachment to molecular asymmetry unclear
- **Correlation vs Causation**: Does attachment cause asymmetry, or do both relate to cell cycle stage?

**Epistemic Status:**
- **Established**: Surface attachment affects asymmetry
- **Speculative**: Mechanism linking attachment to molecular asymmetry
- **Plausible but unproven**: Attachment as physical trigger

**Recommendation:** Frame as "attachment influences asymmetry" with mechanistic uncertainty acknowledged.

---

### 1.4 Stalked Pole Distinctiveness

**Correlations Documented:**
- Stalked pole has distinct membrane composition (Gora et al., 2023)
- Cell wall synthesis machinery localizes to stalked pole (Kuru et al., 2017)
- Biochemical asymmetry exists

**Causal Evidence:**
- **CHICKEN-AND-EGG PROBLEM**: Does biochemical asymmetry cause physical asymmetry, or vice versa?
- **Likely bidirectional**: Molecular and physical asymmetries reinforce each other
- **Not a pure physical input**: Biochemical asymmetry IS molecular asymmetry

**Epistemic Status:**
- **Established**: Stalked pole is biochemically distinct
- **Unclear**: Causal priority (physical → molecular or molecular → physical)

**Recommendation:** Frame as "bidirectional coupling" not "physical causes molecular."

## Part 5: Language Recommendations

### 5.1 Terms to Use

**For correlations:**
- "Correlates with"
- "Is associated with"
- "Co-occurs with"
- "Spatially overlaps with"

**For speculative mechanisms:**
- "May contribute to"
- "Has been proposed to"
- "One hypothesis is that"
- "The field is currently debating whether"

**For established mechanisms:**
- "Is required for"
- "Establishes"
- "Creates"
- "Determines"

**For uncertainty:**
- "Remains unclear"
- "Is not yet established"
- "Requires further investigation"
- "The mechanism is unknown"

---

### 5.2 Terms to Avoid

**Overstating physical evidence:**
- ❌ "Senses curvature" → ✅ "Localizes to curved regions"
- ❌ "Detects surface attachment" → ✅ "Responds to surface attachment"
- ❌ "Mechanistic link" → ✅ "Correlation"

**Implying causation without evidence:**
- ❌ "Due to" → ✅ "Associated with"
- ❌ "Driven by" → ✅ "May be influenced by"
- ❌ "Triggers" → ✅ "Correlates with"

## Part 6: Specific Text Revisions

### Current Text (Problematic):

"**Physical Aspects of Asymmetry:**

Recent work reveals that physical cues contribute to initial symmetry breaking:

- **PopZ localization**: PopZ forms polymeric structures at cell poles and may sense membrane curvature (Bowman et al., 2019)
- **DivJ/PleC membrane localization**: These polar kinases localize to specific membrane curvatures, coupling physical geometry to asymmetric signaling (Gora et al., 2023)
- **Surface attachment effects**: Physical attachment to surfaces influences initial symmetry breaking (Jenson et al., 2022)"

---

### Revised Text (Epistemically Careful):

"**Potential Physical Contributions to Symmetry Breaking:**

Physical cues have been proposed to contribute to initial symmetry breaking, though the mechanistic basis remains uncertain:

- **PopZ at poles**: PopZ forms polymeric structures at cell poles, which have high membrane curvature (Bowman et al., 2019). However, whether this represents active curvature sensing versus passive accumulation at curved regions is debated (Lasker & Gitai, 2022).

- **DivJ/PleC localization patterns**: DivJ localizes to the stalked pole (convex curvature) while PleC localizes to the swarmer pole (concave curvature) (Gora et al., 2023; Curtis & Brun, 2022). This spatial asymmetry correlates with membrane curvature, but it is unclear whether curvature determines localization or both reflect molecular landmarks.

- **Surface attachment**: Surface attachment influences asymmetry establishment (Jenson et al., 2022), but the causal mechanisms linking attachment to molecular asymmetry are not fully understood.

**Important caveat**: None of these physical correlations currently rise to the level of established sensing mechanisms. The field expresses significant uncertainty about whether physical cues are actively detected (sensing) versus passively correlated with molecular asymmetry. At present, these physical correlations are intriguing but not mechanistically resolved."
