# Caulobacter Peer Review Response: Quick Reference

**Date:** 2026-04-23
**Issue:** Section 4.1 conflates correlation with mechanism regarding physical aspects of Caulobacter asymmetry

---

## The Core Problem

Peer review concern: "If none of these physical correlations rise to the level of established sensing mechanisms, their inclusion in the 'Physical Aspects of Asymmetry' subsection risks giving more weight to physical contributions in Caulobacter than the evidence warrants."

---

## Solution: Reframing That Strengthens the Example

**Key Insight:** Acknowledging uncertainty about physical inputs actually STRENGTHENS the hierarchical framework by demonstrating molecular autonomy.

---

## Evidence Categorization

### Established (Well-Supported)
- CckA-ChpT-CtrA phosphorelay controls asymmetric cell fate
- DivJ/PleC localization patterns (stalked vs swarmer pole)
- c-di-GMP signaling establishes stalked-cell identity
- Asymmetric division produces distinct cell fates
- Molecular asymmetry PRECEDES developmental asymmetry

### Correlation (Observed but Mechanism Unclear)
- PopZ localizes to curved poles (but does it "sense" curvature?)
- DivJ at convex curvature, PleC at concave curvature (but does curvature cause this?)
- Surface attachment influences asymmetry (but how?)
- Stalked pole has distinct composition (but cause or consequence?)

### Speculative (Not Established)
- Active membrane curvature sensing by PopZ or DivJ/PleC
- Surface attachment as physical trigger for symmetry breaking
- Physical asymmetry preceding molecular asymmetry

---

## Language Changes

### Replace
- ❌ "Senses curvature" → ✅ "Localizes to curved regions"
- ❌ "Detects surface attachment" → ✅ "Responds to surface attachment"
- ❌ "Mechanistic link" → ✅ "Correlation"
- ❌ "Driven by" → ✅ "May be influenced by"

### Add
- "The mechanistic basis remains uncertain"
- "It is unclear whether"
- "The field expresses significant uncertainty about"
- "Current evidence supports correlation but not causation"

---

## Reframing Strategy

### OLD FRAME: Physical cues initiate asymmetry → Molecular systems amplify it
**Problem:** Overstates physical evidence

### NEW FRAME: Molecular systems create asymmetry autonomously → Physical cues (if present) modulate timing/location
**Strength:** Emphasizes molecular autonomy while allowing physical inputs as optional

---

## Key Messages for Revision

1. **Molecular autonomy:** The phosphorelay can break symmetry de novo through positive feedback and bistability

2. **Physical inputs are optional:** The system works with or without physical cues

3. **Uncertainty is a strength:** Demonstrating molecular autonomy despite physical input uncertainty strengthens the hierarchical framework claim

4. **Epistemic care:** Distinguishing established from speculative is a virtue, not a weakness

---

## Revised Section Structure

1. **The Phenomenon** (unchanged)
2. **Molecular Symmetry Breaking** (NEW - emphasize autonomous capacity)
3. **Potential Physical Contributions** (revised with caveats)
   - Each physical aspect: correlation established, mechanism speculative
   - Explicit epistemic assessment
4. **Hierarchical Integration** (reframed)
   - Molecular autonomy emphasized
   - Physical modulation as optional
   - Uncertainty strengthens rather than weakens

---

## Bottom Line

**The revision should NOT retreat from Caulobacter as an example. Instead, reframe to show that molecular systems can create asymmetry autonomously, making this an EVEN BETTER demonstration of molecular override than if they merely responded to physical cues.**
