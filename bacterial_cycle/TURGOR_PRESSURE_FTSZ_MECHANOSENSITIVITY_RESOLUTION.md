## Executive Summary

The reviewer identified that the claim in Section 2.1 that "FtsZ polymerization can be influenced by membrane tension and curvature" cites Loose & Mitchison (2014) and Bisson-Filho et al. (2017), but the framing suggests this is evidence for physiologically relevant FtsZ mechanosensitivity. The reviewer noted that Loose & Mitchison is an in vitro liposome reconstitution study, and the in vivo evidence for turgor or membrane tension directly modulating FtsZ assembly in living cells remains very limited. The caveat was noted downstream but should be stated more clearly at the point of the claim.

**Solution Implemented**: Revised the FtsZ mechanosensitivity bullet point in Section 2.1 to include clear caveats at the point of the claim itself, distinguishing between in vitro and in vivo evidence and noting the uncertainty about physiological relevance.

## The Core Problem Identified by the Reviewer

### The Issue

**Problematic statement** (Section 2.1, line 138):
> "- **FtsZ mechanosensitivity**: Recent work shows FtsZ polymerization can be influenced by membrane tension and curvature (Loose & Mitchison, 2014; Bisson-Filho et al., 2017)"

**The problem**:
1. **Loose & Mitchison (2014)**: This is an **in vitro** liposome reconstitution study, not in vivo
2. **Bisson-Filho et al. (2017)**: Reported membrane tension sensitivity in vivo, but this finding remains debated
3. **Framing issue**: The statement suggests FtsZ mechanosensitivity is physiologically relevant
4. **Caveat location**: The manuscript noted limitations downstream (in "Limitations" section), but not at the point of the claim

**Reviewer's point**: "The in vivo evidence for turgor or membrane tension directly modulating FtsZ assembly in living cells remains very limited and the paper should say so more clearly at the point of the claim rather than in a downstream caveat."

---

## Solution Implemented: Clear Caveat at Point of Claim

### Revised Statement (Section 2.1, line 138)

**BEFORE** (Problematic):
> "- **FtsZ mechanosensitivity**: Recent work shows FtsZ polymerization can be influenced by membrane tension and curvature (Loose & Mitchison, 2014; Bisson-Filho et al., 2017)"

**AFTER** (Corrected):
> "- **FtsZ mechanosensitivity** (limited in vivo evidence): In vitro studies using liposome reconstitution show FtsZ polymerization can be influenced by membrane tension and curvature (Loose & Mitchison, 2014). Bisson-Filho et al. (2017) reported membrane tension sensitivity in vivo, but this finding remains debated and the physiological relevance of FtsZ mechanosensitivity in living cells is **not well established**. **Important caveat**: The in vivo evidence for turgor pressure or membrane tension directly modulating FtsZ assembly in living bacterial cells remains very limited. Most evidence for FtsZ mechanosensitivity comes from in vitro systems, and whether FtsZ functions as a mechanosensor in physiological conditions remains uncertain."
