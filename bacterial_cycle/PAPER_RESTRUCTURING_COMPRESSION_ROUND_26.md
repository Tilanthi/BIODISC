### Phase 3: Narrative Conversion (Bullet Points → Prose)

**Style Transformation Principles**:

**FROM (Bullet-point style)**:
```markdown
**Key Findings:**
- Purified FtsZ forms dynamic patterns and Z-rings
- Minimal systems can recapitulate aspects of division
- Self-organization doesn't equal physiological regulation

**Limitations:**
- Reconstituted systems lack complexity
- Require non-physiological conditions
```

**TO (Narrative prose)**:
```markdown
In vitro reconstitution experiments demonstrate that purified FtsZ can form dynamic patterns and Z-ring structures in liposomes (Osawa & Erickson, 2013; Ramirez-Diaz et al., 2021), and minimal systems can recapitulate certain aspects of division (García et al., 2021). However, these reconstituted systems typically lack the complexity of living cells and often require non-physiological conditions, limiting their ability to inform our understanding of physiological regulation. Moreover, the observation of self-organization in simplified systems does not establish that such mechanisms operate in vivo.
```

**Conversion Rules**:
1. Replace bullet lists with connected sentences
2. Use transitional phrases ("However," "Moreover," "Furthermore")
3. Group related points in single paragraphs
4. Maintain scientific precision while improving flow
5. Reserve bullet points ONLY for:
   - Critical numbered lists (1, 2, 3)
   - Essential predictions where clarity matters more than style
