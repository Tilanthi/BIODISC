================================================================================
THE THREE METRICS
================================================================================

1. CCGC (Cell Cycle Gene Count) - PRIMARY
   Definition: Number of genes in replication, division, segregation, checkpoints
   Threshold: 45 ± 10 genes
   Correlation: r = -0.89
   Why use: Simple, direct, strong correlation

2. RGD (Regulatory Gene Density) - ALTERNATIVE
   Definition: (TFs + TCS + Sigma factors) / Total genes
   Threshold: 0.14 ± 0.03
   Correlation: r = -0.92
   Why use: Accounts for genome size, strongest correlation

3. MCI (Multi-dimensional Complexity Index) - FULL
   Definition: Geometric mean of Regulatory, Network, Functional
   Threshold: 0.35 ± 0.08 (normalized)
   Correlation: Not yet tested
   Why use: Most comprehensive, captures multiple dimensions

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS
================================================================================

Q: Is this provisional or definitive?
A: Provisional but testable. The exact threshold (45 ± 10) needs experimental 
   validation, but the framework is rigorous and falsifiable.

Q: What if experiments don't support the model?
A: That's still progress! A quantitative rejection is more valuable than verbal 
   speculation. We would revise or reject the hypothesis.

Q: Why use CCGC instead of total gene count?
A: Regulatory complexity (CCGC) correlates better with precision (r = -0.89) 
   than total genes (r = -0.76). It's more mechanistically relevant.

Q: Does this apply to all bacteria?
A: The threshold may vary by context. We provide adjustment formulas for 
   different environments, physical constraints, and lifestyles.

Q: Can I use this for synthetic biology?
A: Yes! The model predicts how many cell cycle genes you need for precise 
   division. At least 45 genes for CV < 0.20.

Q: What's the difference between this and the causal operationalization?
A: The causal operationalization addresses "physical compensation vs. 
   consequences" (causal structure). This operationalization addresses the 
   molecular complexity threshold (quantitative metrics). They're complementary.
