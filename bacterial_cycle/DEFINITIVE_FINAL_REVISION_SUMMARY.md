#### 9. CAULOBACTER SYMMETRY-BREAKING NUANCE ADDED ✅ FULLY RESOLVED

**Problem:** Overclaim that asymmetry is entirely molecularly determined

**New Subsection: "Nuance - The Role of Physical Asymmetry in Symmetry Breaking"**

**Content:**
> "It is debated in the *Caulobacter* field whether physical asymmetry (the stalk attachment point and associated membrane curvature) contributes to initial symmetry breaking, with molecular systems then amplifying and maintaining this asymmetry. Some evidence suggests:
> - The stalked pole has distinct membrane composition (Gora et al., 2023)
> - Cell wall synthesis machinery localizes asymmetrically (Kuru et al., 2017)
> - Physical attachment to surfaces may influence the initial symmetry-breaking event (Jenson et al., 2022; Persat et al., 2014)

> **What is clear**: Whether the initial trigger involves physical asymmetry or not, the **developmental outcome**—asymmetric division producing distinct cell fates with different replication competence—cannot be explained by physical constraints alone."

**Added References:**
- Kuru et al. (2017) on cell wall synthesis
- Persat et al. (2014) on surface attachment
